Great products are not defined by their architecture or features. They're defined by the details — the micro-interactions, the attention to edge cases, the polish that turns functional software into something people enjoy using.

## Micro-interactions

The 200ms animation on a button press. The subtle shadow on a card. The perfectly timed tooltip. These micro-interactions communicate care and build trust with users.

## Edge Cases Are Where Quality Lives

The happy path is easy. The unhappy path is where good products distinguish themselves:

- What happens when the network is slow?
- How does the form behave with invalid input?
- What does the error message say?

## Consistency Builds Trust

When every button follows the same pattern, every dialog has the same behavior, and every page follows the same layout, users develop an intuition for how your product works. This intuition is trust.

## The Standard You Walk Past

Every detail you ignore sets a standard for the next one. Small inconsistencies compound. Fix the small things, and the big things follow.
